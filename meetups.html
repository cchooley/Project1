Meetups

Not what you know, but who you know, as it were. Building out a network of contacts in the tech world is so crucial to short and long term success that Galvanize actually made attendance mandatory (complete with written reflections). Here are 3 of mine.

First Meetup: Let's Learn Some Python (2/26/18)

People I met - Met Steve, who has worked in coding for 19 years specializing in DBA, Oracle and mySQL. He is currently a contractor with Comcast. Comes to several of these. I also met Brian, who ran the class and is an instructor with Devetry. Seemed like a really good instructor.

What I learned - The meet up was structured around building a hangman game with Python, and this was my first time ever using the code. So everything was definitely new! That said, I’d say my main takeaways were with Python syntax, variables, loops and if/elif/else statements. We had about an hour and a half to write the game out and barely made it through before the library closed at 8. It was very interesting to see a task I had already performed in the pre-course activities (building a hangman game in Javascript) replicated with a brand new language. A nice introductory point for me, I think.

Questions - I have so many questions! Unsurprisingly I can’t help but compare Python to Javascript and the main thing sticking out to me is: does Python have a generally simpler and cleaner syntax than Javascript? My inital reaction is yes. But also, what are the ideal tasks for using Python? And is there really this big Python v. Javascript rivalry that the instructor seemed to imply? That’s really just the tip of the iceberg regarding my curiosity, but I’ll leave it there for now.

Second Meetup - Front Range Front-End: CSS Basics - Selectors (3/1/18)

People I met - James Steinbach - UX Developer, DockYard; Dano Manion - Owner of Mahtay.com

What I learned - I have to admit that this one didn't broaden my horizons that much since I've probably advanced past the point of needing a referendum on CSS selectors. Interestingly (or maybe expectedly?) the newest material dealt with learning selectors like 'of-type' that generally aren't used in production (at least according to James). Now, having said that, the last 10 minutes or so that covered some methods of how to use CSS to hack websites was very new and interesting from a technical standpoint.

Questions - Questions relating to the meetup content would probably be focused on use cases for some of the lesser-known or used selectors. We did get a few but it was very cursory. And a question that is more related to the nature of meetups moreso than the content of last night's would be: when is a lecture-style meetup appropriate, vs. a code-along style meetup, vs. a socially-driven meetup? It was admittedly very dry to sit through this topic for an hour-and-a-half, and I definitely felt like it would have been more appropriate to at least break the lecture up with opportunities for the attendees to practice and/or implement what was being learned.

Third Meetup: March Develop Happy Hour (3/13/18)

People I met: Quentin Hartman - Director of Infrastructure & DevOps at Finalze; Joe Simon - Promoter, Go Code Colorado

What I Learned - What sticks out is the conversation I had with Quentin regarding his company's approach to hiring and how it perceives developers coming out of the Galvanize program (or similar programs). He generally thought highly of Galvanize students, because they are entering the field with demonstrable experience and have already displayed a high level of initiative by completing the course (particularly as the culmination of a major career change). Apart from that, it was interesting to hear someone with his perspective speak to the continual need for new developers in the field, as well as how junior developers coming out of programs like Galvanize tend to work a little cheaper than others - an obvious plus for them.

Setting that aside, I can't say I acquired a wealth of knowledge. But it was definitely cool to hang out with quite a few developers in a relaxed and casual setting. I also have to say I was impressed by all that there was to do - lots of games, for sure! Exceeded my expectations there.

Questions - I suppose the biggest question I have is how useful these events directly are for getting hired when one eventually is a legitimate candidate for employment. I'm obviously a ways away yet, but when that day does arrive will it be realistic for me to look at these as a practical means of getting hired? Or just a bit of fun? Nothing wrong with the latter, of course! It was a pretty good time.

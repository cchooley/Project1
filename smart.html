SMART Goals

"SMART" Goals? Is that an ackronym or something?

Pretty much, yeah. In a nutshell, it's a method of setting goals that are:

  • S - Specific
  • M - Measurable
  • A - Attainable
  • R - Realistic
  • T - Timely

Why do they matter? Well, you use SMART Goals to write better to-do lists. And I'm a big fan of to-do lists.

To-do lists should push us to do our most important work by way of telling us how to get that work done. They should also be designed to tell you what to do next after a task is completed.

So at the start of each day, the idea is that you're setting goals that adhere to the 5 SMART principles above. In doing so, you're setting benchmarks that are ambitious but sensible - you're not cheating yourself by lining up a bunch of easy tasks (and then thinking that completing them means you accomplished something), but you're also not setting yourself up for disappointment by creating unreasonable expectations you simply can't meet.

SMART Goals should derive from one big, ambitious "stretch" goal. Ideally, they should take that goal and break it down into smaller, achievable components.
